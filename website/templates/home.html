Background


These days, cardiovascular diseases is the main cause of death around the world. Coronary artery disease is due to the blockage of arteries when cholesterol and fatty deposits adhere to the wall of arteries and restrict the blood flow. This causes abnormal artery functions. The heart needs more oxygen and vital nutrients to work properly. Due to the lack of normal blood flow, many people experience chest pain referred to as angina. Currently the best and most accurate method for diagnosing angina is angiography. However angiography can be complex and expensive. Researchers are therefore looking for more affordable, simple but still robust and reliable method to predict the occurrence of angina in patients.

Goals


The main goal of this project is to develop a predictive model. First, we are trying to use a feature selection to find the most importance features for all subjects. This should be done with several feature selection algorithms. The second goal is to identify predictors (variables) that have the most impact on successful categorization. In this step, students will investigate and evaluate various classifier models and select ones that they think best suit their requirements. Students should be able to discuss and justify decisions made.

Explain prediction models


We provide 6 models: SVM, KNN, LDA, SVM 2, Voting, Voting 2


  • SVM, KNN and LDA models are implemented using Bagging method with K-cross validation method. The Bagging method helps our training model reduce the variance of accuracy while using K-corss validation method

  • SVM 2 model is implemented using Bagging method with K-cross validation method. But it is trained on input that is the prediction result of SVM, LDA, KNN models

  • Voting model is implemented using Voting method to improve accuracy and the models list for Voting method includes SVM, KNN, LDA

  • Voting model is implemented using Voting method to improve accuracy and the models list for Voting method includes SVM, KNN, LDA but on the input that is result of SVM, KNN, LDA models
